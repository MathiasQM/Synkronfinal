BY ASBJØRN KRAGH

Bank of materials

Process:

In this project we set out to compile and expand upon the graphical elements of the visual identity for BOM. The goals of the design had a multitude of angels and so it was important for us to establish a deep understanding of Bank Of Materials and its users through a range of meetings and extended dialogs. Our findings were:

On one level of participation the goal of the design served as a concrete way to guide internal stakeholders on ways to share and access materials. On another level the goal was to onboard new collaborators onto the concept and to gain a better understanding of the concept and organization of BOM and on ways to share, support and collaborate.

In order to reach these goals we took an editorial design approach, creating user manuals, concept descriptions and compilations of graphical assets, to organize and shape the story of BOM, focusing on the user experience and keeping the different levels of participation in mind.

Finally Bank of Materials is also a physical material bank of the Architecture School of Aarhus, and so wayfinding and onsite communication was of particular importance. To further the ease of use and participation we connected the editorial design, projects of BOM and brand narrative to the site by designing a range of signage in the form of banners, flags, labels and more.

Products:

The end product was a series of digital and graphic designs working to make the message of Bank of Materials accessible and visible for a number of different partners and stakeholders.

This consisted of both editorial design, layouting and compilations of digital assets of the BOM identity, as well as physical large scale graphical material for wayfinding and onsite communication.
